About the artist

Sam Ford is an illustrator, visual artist and musician operating out of Los Angeles by way of Portland, OR. He has worked since 2005 creating album covers, logos, merchandise, and show flyers for local, national and international acts, as well as designing tattoos, murals, and comic illustration, all while playing drums and singing in the band Wizard Rifle. He was born in San Francisco, raised to the age of 5 in the redwoods of Humboldt County, then lived until 18 years old on San Juan Island in Washington State. It was here that legendary artist, writer and comic industry veteran Paul Chadwick took Sam on as an apprentice for nearly six years and lent his limitless talent and wisdom (and patience) to passing on the art of illustration and storytelling.

In 2005 Sam moved to Portland Oregon, became part of the thriving and diverse music scene there, and spent the better part of the next 5 years doing work for local and touring bands, in particular those aligned with the heavier and darker side of music.

With the help of many great and supportive friends over the years, such as frequent collaborator Nate Carson of Witch Mountain and Nanotear Booking, Sam has managed to create a large body of work covering many different styles and media for an ever-growing range of clients.

Sam has exhibited both original and commissioned works at the Hive Gallery in downtown Los Angeles (http://hivegallery.com/2010/), as well as galleries in NYC and Portland.
